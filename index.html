Biography

Olympian. International Coach. Program Leader.

Florencia Cerutti is an Olympian and internationally recognized sailing coach with extensive experience in high-performance athlete development, competitive team leadership, coach education, and long-term program growth.

Born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, Cerutti built her athletic career in Olympic single-handed classes and represented Paraguay at the 2008 Olympic Games in Beijing, becoming the first Paraguayan woman to compete in Olympic sailing.

Throughout her competitive career, she achieved major continental results and established herself as a respected athlete in South American and international sailing environments.

After her Olympic campaign, she moved to Barcelona and began an international coaching career that expanded across clubs, federations, development programs, and high-performance sailing environments in Europe, the Americas, and beyond.

Her work extends beyond on-the-water coaching. Cerutti has led racing programs, supported athlete pathways, trained and mentored coaches, coordinated regatta logistics, and contributed to the long-term growth of competitive sailing structures.

Olympic Athlete

Olympic Athlete Career

Florencia Cerutti represented Paraguay in the Laser Radial class at the 2008 Olympic Games in Beijing, becoming the first Paraguayan woman to compete in Olympic sailing.

Her Olympic campaign was the result of years of international competition in single-handed dinghy classes, where she developed the technical precision, tactical awareness, and resilience that later shaped her coaching philosophy.

Throughout her competitive career, Cerutti raced internationally across Europe and the Americas, building experience in elite regattas and major championships.

Her athletic journey includes a bronze medal at the 2003 Pan American Games and a broader competitive pathway that established the foundations for her transition into international coaching.

Current Role

Head Coach & Assistant Sailing Director

Florencia Cerutti currently serves as Head Coach and Assistant Sailing Director at Norfolk Yacht & Country Club in Virginia, United States.

In this role she oversees youth racing programs, supports athlete development pathways, and contributes to the structure and growth of competitive sailing within one of the region’s most active sailing communities.

Her responsibilities include on-water coaching, program planning, coach mentorship, regatta preparation, and the development of training environments that support both performance and long-term sailor progression.
